> [!tldr]
> ETS researcher and first author of the 2012 paper proposing a five-area evaluation framework for automated scoring.

## Overview

> [!source] Williamson is based at Educational Testing Service in Princeton, NJ.
> Listed affiliation in [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]: "David M. Williamson, Xiaoming Xi, and F. Jay Breyer, Educational Testing Service, Rosedale Road, Princeton, NJ 08541; dmwilliamson@ets.org." [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

> [!source] Williamson has published on automated scoring across multiple domains beyond essay scoring.
> Co-authored work on automated scoring of architectural design (Williamson, Bejar, & Hone, 1999), spoken-response scoring with SpeechRater (Xi, Higgins, Zechner, & Williamson, 2008), and essay-score validity (Bridgeman, Trapani, & Williamson, 2011), and edited the volume "Automated scoring of complex tasks in computer based testing." [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]
