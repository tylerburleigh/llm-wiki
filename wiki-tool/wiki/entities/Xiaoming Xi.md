> [!tldr]
> ETS researcher and second author of Williamson, Xi, and Breyer (2012); her prior validity-framework work for automated scoring shapes the argument-based structure of that paper.

## Overview

> [!source] Xi's earlier work underpins the paper's validity structure.
> The authors credit Xi with applying "a more elaborated framework for evaluating the use of automated scoring for a speaking practice test" (Xi et al., 2008) and with explicating "the unique validity issues introduced by automated scoring to each of the inferences contributing to the overall validity argument for the assessment." [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

> [!source] Xi has published on automated scoring validity, speech scoring, and test fairness.
> Publications cited in the paper include Xi (2008, 2010a, 2010b, in press) and Xi, Higgins, Zechner, & Williamson (2008) on SpeechRater. [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

> [!source] Xi's operational definition of fairness shapes the framework.
> The paper adopts Xi's definition of fairness as "comparable validity for *identifiable* and *relevant* groups" (Xi, 2010b, p. 154) to justify applying selected evaluation criteria to subgroups. [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

## Relationships

- Co-author with [[David M Williamson]] and [[F Jay Breyer]] on the framework paper.
- Researcher at [[Educational Testing Service]].
- Primary author on the validity-inference approach threaded through the [[Automated Scoring Evaluation Framework]].
