> [!tldr]
> US-based assessment organization that developed and operates the e-rater automated essay scoring system and authored the framework paper illustrating its use in high-stakes testing.

## Overview

> [!source] ETS is based in Princeton, New Jersey.
> The author affiliation block lists "Educational Testing Service, Rosedale Road, Princeton, NJ 08541." [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

> [!source] ETS was the first organization to deploy automated essay scoring for high-stakes assessment.
> "As the first to deploy automated scoring of essays for high-stakes assessment with the GMAT in 1999 and with multiple implementations in consequential assessments for other populations, such as TOEFL and GRE, ETS has developed a general framework for implementation in consequential assessment." [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

> [!source] ETS runs a large interdisciplinary team on e-rater.
> "A team of more than 15 research scientists, including natural language processing (NLP) scientists, computational linguists, cognitive scientists, and psychometricians, have contributed to e-rater development and evaluation, as have computer engineers, data analysts, and research assistants. Development is also overseen by technical and subject matter advisory teams consisting of psychometric and content experts." [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

> [!source] ETS has published more than 20 peer-reviewed papers on e-rater since 1998.
> The paper cites "more than 20 peer-reviewed publications that document various aspects of the quality of e-rater including construct representation, reliability, relationships with other criteria of writing ability, and consequences of using e-rater." [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

> [!source] ETS upgrades e-rater annually.
> Engine upgrades follow a component sign-off, full engine evaluation, and full model rebuild cycle, and "currently [occur] on an annual basis." [[Williamson Xi Breyer 2012 - Framework for Automated Scoring]]

> [!analysis] ETS occupies a dual role as system builder and framework author.
> The paper's framework doubles as a retrospective rationalization of ETS's existing practice: every concrete threshold (0.70 kappa, 0.10 degradation, 0.15 / 0.10 mean-difference flags, 0.5 / 1.5 discrepancy) reflects ETS internal policy. Readers should treat the framework as one organization's best practice surfaced as generalizable guidance, rather than independent third-party review.

## Relationships

- Developer of [[e-rater]].

- Operates e-rater within GRE (Issue, Argument) and TOEFL (Independent, Integrated) Writing programs.
- Formerly powered GMAT essay scoring (until GMAT migrated to IntelliMetric).
